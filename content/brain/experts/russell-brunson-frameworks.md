# Russell Brunson — frameworks for founders (Danny synthesis)

Public frameworks from Brunson's teaching, applied to personal-brand founders. Not book text — operational moves.

## Value ladder

Every business needs ascension: free → low ticket → core offer → premium.

For personal brands: LinkedIn content is the **free layer**. Workshop or challenge is **low ticket**. Done-with-you branding is **core**. 1:1 or intensive is **premium**.

**Diagnostic:** If they're only posting and never ascending, they have a traffic problem disguised as a content problem.

## Hook — Story — Offer

Every piece of content and every sales conversation follows:

1. **Hook** — pattern interrupt. Specific pain or contrarian claim. Not "5 tips for LinkedIn."
2. **Story** — relatable bridge. Founder moment, client archetype, or your own failure. Proof without flexing.
3. **Offer** — one clear next step. DM, call, workshop seat, lead magnet.

**Move:** Rewrite their last post as HSO. If there's no offer, it's entertainment — fine for reach, useless for pipeline.

## Perfect Webinar structure (compressed)

For live workshops and VSL-style pitches:

1. **Origin story** — why you, why now (vulnerable, not hero journey slop).
2. **Three secrets** — each breaks a false belief about the vehicle, internal ability, or external excuse.
3. **Stack + close** — what they get, what it costs to wait, one CTA.

Maps directly to Purely Personal workshop Day 1 → Day 2 close.

## Funnel thinking

A funnel is not a website. It's **the ordered steps a stranger takes to become a buyer**.

Minimum viable funnel for founders: content → DM or lead magnet → discovery call → offer.

**Question to ask:** Where do people fall off? Fix that step before adding tools.

## Dream customer

Brunson's "dream customer" = Danny's ICP with a story attached. Who are they at 11pm when they can't sleep? What did they already try?

Always tie content hooks to that night's thought, not demographics alone.
