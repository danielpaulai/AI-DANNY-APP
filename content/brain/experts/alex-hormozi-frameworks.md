# Alex Hormozi — frameworks for founders (Danny synthesis)

Hormozi-style offer and acquisition thinking, translated for personal-brand operators. Framework names only — original application copy.

## Value equation

Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

**Use it live on calls:** Ask which variable is killing the offer. Usually it's **effort** (they don't believe they can execute) or **likelihood** (no proof).

**Fix moves:**
- Raise dream outcome specificity ("5 inbound calls/month" beats "grow your brand").
- Add proof (screenshots, process, guarantees on process not results where legal).
- Shorten time delay (90-day sprint vs "eventually").
- Reduce effort (done-with-you, templates, AI employee).

## Grand Slam Offer

Make the offer so good they'd feel stupid saying no:

1. **Attractive avatar** — narrow ICP.
2. **Premium price** — price is a filter; cheap attracts nightmares.
3. **Unmatchable value** — stack components with named dollar values (anchor, don't invent fake totals).
4. **Risk reversal** — guarantee the **process** or milestone, not fantasy ROI.

For PB clients: stack voice profile + content system + workshop + skills + office hours. Name each component.

## Lead magnets that actually convert

Good lead magnet = **quick win toward the core problem**, consumable in <15 minutes.

Bad: 47-page PDF nobody opens.

Examples for founders: ICP one-pager, voice snapshot, invisibility score on a post, "first week content calendar."

## More, better, new

Acquisition order: do **more** of what works, make it **better**, then try **new** channels.

Founders violate this by jumping to new platforms when their LinkedIn DM follow-up is broken.

## Pricing courage

"If you're not uncomfortable with the price, it's too low" — for established operators with proof.

For early-stage: price to **filter** and **fund delivery**, not to impress.
