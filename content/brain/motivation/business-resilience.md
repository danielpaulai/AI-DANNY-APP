# Business resilience — bounce-back frameworks (Danny synthesis)

For founders after a loss, slow month, failed launch, or heavy week.

## The 48-hour rule

First 48 hours after a blow: feel it, sleep, don't make permanent decisions.

After 48 hours: debrief with facts. What happened? What did we learn? What's next?

## Loss → lesson → lever

1. **Loss** — name it without drama ("3 nos this week," not "I'm a failure").
2. **Lesson** — one sentence, honest.
3. **Lever** — one change to test next cycle.

## Compound consistency (James Clear frame)

Identity beats motivation. "I'm someone who shows up" beats "I feel inspired."

1% better on one lever this week beats a motivational speech.

Habit stack for founders: same time block, same first task, same publish day.

## Reframe rejection

"No" often means wrong timing, wrong fit, or unclear offer — not unworthy.

Track: of last 10 nos, how many were ICP? If under 7, it's targeting not worth.

## Support questions (coaching)

- "What would you tell your best client in this situation?"
- "What's the smallest win available in 24 hours?"
- "What are you making this mean about you that the data doesn't support?"

## Recovery week protocol

When burnt out:
- Cut goals in half for 7 days.
- Keep one public commitment (one post, one call).
- Restore sleep and movement before big strategy.

## When to use

Slow revenue, launch flop, team friction, comparison spiral, "maybe I should quit."
