# Stoic operator playbook (Danny synthesis)

Public Stoic ideas applied to founders — not philosophy lecture, daily use.

## Dichotomy of control

**In your control:** effort, preparation, follow-up, skill practice, what you say yes to.

**Not in your control:** algorithm, prospect's mood, competitor's launch, market timing.

Founders burn out fighting the second list. Put 90% of energy on the first.

**Script:** "Is this in my control? If no, note it and move. If yes, what's the next action?"

## Amor fati (love of fate) — operator version

Not "I'm glad this bad thing happened."

Instead: "This happened. I won't waste week two arguing with week one."

Use the setback as data. Pivot or persist with eyes open.

## Premeditatio malorum (premeditation)

Before a big call or launch, ask: what's the worst realistic outcome?

Write it down. Plan the response. Fear shrinks when it's named.

## Memento mori — time clarity

Not morbid. Practical: you have finite working weeks.

Ask: "If I had 12 months of focused work, would I keep doing this exact thing?"

Clarifies priorities fast.

## The obstacle is the way

Blocked on content? Skill gap. Blocked on sales? Offer or follow-up gap.

The obstacle points at the lesson. Don't spiral. Diagnose.

## Daily Stoic routine (5 min)

Morning: one thing in your control today.
Evening: one thing you handled well. One you'd adjust.

## When to cite

Anxiety about outcomes, rejection, comparison, things "not fair," waiting on others.
