## The core principle

The weeks after signing are where clients either build momentum or drift. Week 2-4 rhythm isn't admin. It's the actual work that proves the engagement was worth the money.

## Why this matters

Most agencies lose clients in the second month. Not because the offer was wrong. Because the energy from Week 1 wore off and nothing replaced it.

The founder goes back to running their business. They open Claude once, get a mediocre answer, close it, and never come back. Three weeks pass. They're paying you but nothing shipped. The engagement dies quietly.

The rhythm you set in Weeks 2-4 determines whether the founder owns the system by the end or hands it back to you unfinished.

If you schedule sessions and hope they show up, half won't. If you build the sessions around what already moves in their calendar, attendance solves itself.

If you front-load all the strategy in Week 1, Weeks 2-4 feel like maintenance. If you sequence the work so each session unlocks the next piece, the founder stays bought in.

The cadence has to cost them something to miss. Not because you're punitive. Because the work compounds. Miss Week 2 and Week 3 makes no sense. That's the lock-in.

## How it shows up in client work

Week 2 is always the Voice DNA session. Not positioning. Not strategy. Voice.

The founder shows up having posted inconsistently for months, typed into Claude 47 times, and gotten output that sounds like a LinkedIn bot. Week 2 is where we fix that permanently.

I run a live 60-minute voice capture. Five questions. I record their answers. I transcribe them. I feed them into Claude with a Voice DNA extraction prompt. By end of session they have a 2-page document Claude reads before every output.

The shift is immediate. Before: "Help me write a post about personal branding." Claude gives them LinkedIn slop. After: same prompt, Claude writes in their cadence, their specificity, their turn of phrase.

That one session is the engagement. Everything else builds on it. [[Voice_DNA_Workshop_2024_08_15]]

Week 3 is Content Strategist skill build. This is where we map their business goals to a 90-day content calendar.

Not random posts. A strategy. Every post maps to one of three outcomes: book a call, build authority, nurture the list. If a post doesn't do one of those three, we cut it.

I use the Taki Moore-influenced framework from [[Content_strategy_session_2024_11_06]]. Three constraints: one ICP, one offer, one outcome per quarter. That's it. Founders who try to serve three audiences in 90 days ship nothing. We force the choice in Week 3.

By end of session they have a working Content Strategist skill that takes a business goal ("I need 10 sales calls this month") and returns a content plan with hooks, formats, and CTAs. Not theory. The actual plan.

Week 4 is where the system starts running without me. This is the Writer + Editor skill build.

The founder brings three past posts that performed. We feed them into Claude with the Voice DNA document. Claude analyzes the patterns. Then we build two skills: one that writes net-new posts in that style, one that edits AI slop back into their voice.

The test at end of Week 4: the founder writes a post using the Writer skill, runs it through the Editor skill, posts it, and sends me the link. If they can't do that workflow solo, we didn't finish the job.

The rhythm across all three weeks is the same: book the session for the same day and time each week. Not "let me know what works." Lock it in Week 1. Tuesdays at 3pm, or whatever day they already block for deep work.

Attendance goes to 90%+ when you anchor to their existing calendar. [[Client_onboarding_2025_01_10]]

## Specific examples from the vault

One client in [[Sales_call_2024_06_18]] signed a 6-week personal brand build. Week 1 was smooth. Positioning workshop, nailed the one-liner, walked out confident.

Week 2 he ghosted. Rescheduled twice. When we finally ran the Voice DNA session he admitted he hadn't opened Claude since Week 1. The energy was gone.

I called it out on the session: "You paid for this. You know it works. But you're not treating it like the strategic priority you said it was when you signed."

He pushed back. Said he was "too busy." I said: "You spent 12 hours last week on content. We're compressing that to 2. You're not too busy. You're avoiding the thing that actually moves the business."

That one call reset the engagement. He showed up to every session after that. Week 4 he had the full system running. By Week 6 he'd booked 8 inbound calls from LinkedIn.

The line that landed: "If this doesn't ship, it's not my fault. It's yours." Founders respect the push. [[Breakthrough_call_2024_06_25]]

Another client in [[Cashflow_Inner_Circle_Session_2026_02_06]] came into Week 2 with a perfect Voice DNA draft but zero content calendar. He'd written three posts that week, all spontaneous, none tied to a business goal.

Week 3 was the Content Strategist build. I asked: "What do you need to close this month?" He said 5 clients at <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-START::-->0K each. I said: "Then every post for the next 30 days maps to that. No random thought leadership. No engagement bait. Every post either builds authority for <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-START::-->0K work or books the call."

We built the calendar in the session. 12 posts. Each one had a hook, a CTA, and a metric. By Week 4 he'd shipped 8 of the 12 and booked 3 calls. One closed.

The thing that changed: clarity of outcome. Once he saw the direct line from content to cash, the resistance disappeared. [[Content_to_cash_session_2026_02_13]]

A third client in [[Voice_DNA_Workshop_2024_08_15]] struggled with the Editor skill in Week 4. She could write posts using the Writer skill but every output still sounded slightly off-brand.

I pulled up her Voice DNA document. The problem was obvious. It said "conversational and warm" but her actual voice was sharp and contrarian. The DNA document was aspirational, not real.

I deleted it and ran the voice capture again. Five new questions. This time I told her: "Stop trying to sound like the LinkedIn coaches. Sound like you on a client call when you're telling them they're wrong."

The new DNA document captured that edge. The Editor skill started working immediately. Her posts went from 3/4 on the Invisibility Diagnostic to 4/4. [[Voice_fix_session_2024_09_03]]

The pattern across all three: Week 2-4 is where you catch the gaps. Voice not captured right. Strategy not tied to revenue. Skills not stress-tested with real work. If you wait until Week 5 to diagnose, you've lost them.

## Scripts and exact phrases

**Week 2 opening (Voice DNA session):**
"This session is the engagement. Everything else builds on this. By end of today Claude writes in your voice. Not close to your voice. Your actual voice. If that doesn't happen, we didn't do the job."

**When the founder says they're too busy to do the homework:**
"You spent 12 hours last week on content and got average results. We're compressing that to 2 and getting better output. You're not too busy. You're avoiding the thing that actually moves the business."

**Week 3 opening (Content Strategist session):**
"Every post for the next 90 days maps to one of three outcomes: book a call, build authority, nurture the list. If a post doesn't do one of those, we cut it. What outcome matters most this quarter?"

**When the founder wants to serve multiple audiences:**
"Pick one. You can expand in Q2. Right now we're optimizing for one ICP, one offer, one outcome. Three constraints. The clarity comes from the constraint."

**Week 4 opening (Writer + Editor session):**
"Today's the test. You're going to write a post using the Writer skill, edit it with the Editor skill, and post it. If you can do that solo by end of session, the system works. If not, we rebuild."

**When a client ghosts between Week 1 and Week 2:**
"You signed this because you knew it was the right move. Now you're avoiding it. That's not a calendar problem. That's a commitment problem. Want to fix it or do you want to cancel?"

**Closing every session Week 2-4:**
"Next session is [day] at [time]. Same link. Don't reschedule unless something's on fire. The rhythm matters more than the perfect timing."

## Common mistakes

Biggest mistake: treating Week 2-4 like maintenance. They're not. They're the actual build.

Week 1 is setup. Week 2-4 is where the skills get built, stress-tested, and handed off. If you think Week 1 was the hard part, you lost the client already.

Second mistake: booking sessions "when the client has time." That's code for "whenever they feel like showing up," which is never.

Lock the day and time in Week 1. Same day every week. Same time. If they can't commit to that, they're not ready to work with you. [[Onboarding_standards_2025_01_10]]

Third mistake: not calling out the ghost between Week 1 and Week 2. Founders respect the push. If they reschedule Week 2 and you just send a new Calendly link, you signaled that their time matters more than the work.

The line is: "You rescheduled twice. That tells me this isn't the priority you said it was when you signed. Do you want to keep going or should we pause?" Most of the time they reset immediately. [[Breakthrough_call_2024_06_25]]

Fourth mistake: building the Voice DNA document from a questionnaire instead of live voice capture. Written answers are filtered. Spoken answers are real.

Run the session live. Record it. Transcribe it. Feed the transcript into Claude. The DNA document that comes out sounds like them, not like the version of themselves they think they should be. [[Voice_capture_method_2024_08_20]]

Fifth mistake: letting the founder pick which skill to build first. They'll pick the easiest one or the one that sounds cool. That's not the sequence.

Voice DNA first. Always. Then Content Strategist. Then Writer + Editor. That's the order that compounds. Anything else and they're typing into a tool that doesn't know them yet. [[Skill_build_sequence_2024_11_06]]

## The contrarian read

Most agencies say "we'll handle your content for you." That's the trap.

The founder outsources their voice. The agency becomes the bottleneck. The founder never learns the system. When the retainer ends, they're back to zero.

I say the opposite: you're going to own this. I'm going to build it with you, teach you how it works, and walk away. The system stays with you.

That positioning scares some buyers. They want the agency to do the work. Those aren't my clients.

The clients I want are the ones who say: "I want to own my marketing. I just don't know how to build the machine." That's who Week 2-4 is built for. [[Positioning_the_handoff_2024_07_12]]

Second contrarian take: most coaches front-load all the strategy in Week 1 and spend Weeks 2-6 "implementing." That's backwards.

Week 1 is positioning and structure. The strategy doesn't fully land until Week 3 when you're building the Content Strategist skill and forcing the founder to choose one outcome for the quarter.

Strategy without the forcing function of a calendar and a CTA is just theory. Week 3 is where theory becomes real. [[Strategy_vs_execution_2024_09_10]]

Third: I don't do "office hours" or "check-in calls." Those are fake engagement.

Office hours signal: "Show up if you feel like it." Check-ins signal: "I'm babysitting you." Both train the founder to expect low accountability.

I lock the session. Same day, same time, every week. If they miss it, they reschedule once. Miss twice and I flag it. That's not being rigid. That's protecting the engagement. [[Accountability_structure_2025_01_15]]

## What it looks like when it's working

By end of Week 2, the founder has a Voice DNA document and they've written one post using it. The post sounds like them. That's the proof.

By end of Week 3, they have a 90-day content calendar mapped to one business goal. Every post has a hook, a format, and a CTA. No random posts. All strategy.

By end of Week 4, they've shipped at least 3 posts using the Writer skill and edited them with the Editor skill. The workflow is solo. They don't need me in the room anymore.

The signal it's working: they stop asking "what should I post about?" and start asking "how do I make this post convert better?" That shift happens Week 3 or 4. If it doesn't, the system didn't land. [[System_ownership_2025_02_01]]

Second signal: they book the next session without a reminder. When the founder is treating the engagement like a strategic priority, they don't need chasing.

Third signal: they're using the skills between sessions. Not once. Three or four times. The Writer skill gets used to draft posts. The Editor skill gets used to fix old content. The Content Strategist gets used to plan the next week.

If the skills sit unused between sessions, the handoff didn't happen. [[Skill_usage_tracking_2025_02_10]]

Fourth signal: inbound starts moving by Week 5. Not leads. Conversations. DMs. Profile views. The founder mentions "someone reached out after reading my post." That's the early proof the content is working. [[Early_traction_2025_02_15]]

Fifth signal: the founder stops doubting the system and starts asking how to scale it. "Can I build a skill for LinkedIn comments?" "Can I build one for outreach DMs?" That question shift means they own it. [[Scaling_questions_2025_02_20]]
