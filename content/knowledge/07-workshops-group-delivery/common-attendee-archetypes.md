## The core principle

Every workshop has the same five people in it.

You can call it a masterclass, a group coaching session, a cohort, a live training, or a 90-minute sprint. The packaging doesn't matter. The five archetypes show up every single time, and if you don't design for all five, three of them walk out disappointed and the other two wonder why they paid.

## Why this matters

Most founders design workshops for one person: themselves three years ago.

They build the session around what THEY needed when they were stuck. They assume everyone in the room has the same problem, the same learning style, the same readiness to act. Then they're confused when half the room zones out, two people dominate the chat, and three others ghost the follow-up calls.

The cost of not designing for all five archetypes is measurable. Refund requests. Testimonials that say "good session but not what I expected." Alumni who don't return for the next cohort. Breakout rooms where one person works and four watch. Post-session slacks that go quiet after 48 hours.

The leverage of getting it right is equally measurable. When you design for all five, retention jumps. Referrals triple. Your testimonials stop being generic ("Danny was great!") and start being specific ("I had the clarity I needed by minute 40 and a launched offer by Friday"). Alumni buy the next cohort before you open the cart.

The mechanism is simple: people pay to solve a problem, but they stay because they were SEEN. If the entire workshop speaks to only one archetype, the other four feel invisible. Invisible people don't refer. They don't renew. They don't become case studies.

## How it shows up in client work

I've run workshops for founders at <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-START::-->00K ARR and founders at $5M ARR. I've run them for solo consultants and for leadership teams. I've run them for people who've never posted on LinkedIn and for people with 50K followers. Same five archetypes every single time.

**The Ready-to-Ship Operator.** This person has a working business. They're making revenue. They have clients. They have systems. They're not here to "figure out their niche" or "discover their voice." They're here because one specific lever is stuck and they want someone to fix it in the room. When I ran the Founder OS AI Masterclass cohorts in late 2025, this was Angelin Shalini. She had clients, a team, a pipeline. She wasn't there to learn "what AI can do." She was there to compress 12 hours of weekly content work into 2. She expected a working system by end of session and would've asked for a refund if she didn't get it. Cited from [[Founder_OS_AI_Masterclass_4th_Session_2025_12_06]] and [[Founder_OS_AI_Masterclass_5th_Session_2025_12_13]].

**The Stuck Strategist.** This person has done the reading. They've taken courses. They've bought templates. They know the frameworks. What they don't have is the DECISION. Should I niche down or stay broad? Should I charge $5K or $50K? Should I build a productised service or keep doing custom? They're paralyzed by options. In the Cashflow Inner Circle sessions with Bryant, this archetype shows up every single week. They ask three follow-up questions to every answer because they're hoping someone will just TELL them what to do. The trap is that if you answer their question, they ask another one. The move is to force a decision by Friday and make that decision the only deliverable that matters for them that week. Cited from [[Cashflow_Inner_Circle_Session_2026_02_13]] and [[Cashflow_Inner_Circle_Session_2026_01_23]].

**The Visibility Chaser.** This person is competent at their craft but invisible in the market. They have the skills, the experience, the portfolio. What they don't have is a pipeline. They're posting inconsistently. Their LinkedIn looks fine but books no calls. They're here because they've realized that delivery competence doesn't equal market presence and they need someone to fix the gap. This was the dominant archetype in the Weekly Success Circle sessions I ran in 2023 and 2024. Franziska Niehus, Jai Shaktawat, Asher — all of them could do the work. None of them could articulate what they did in one sentence or hook a post that moved someone to DM. The sessions that worked for them weren't about TEACHING frameworks. They were about APPLYING frameworks to their exact ICP and exact offer until a real post shipped. Cited from [[Weekly_Success_Circle_🌟_2023_09_25]] and [[Weekly_Success_Circle_🌟_2024_01_15]].

**The Confidence-Blocked Executor.** This person knows EXACTLY what to do. They've written the outline. They've drafted the post. They've designed the offer. But they won't ship it. They're waiting for permission, validation, or proof that it won't fail. This archetype dominates 1-on-1 personal branding coaching but shows up in group sessions too. They ask "does this sound okay?" about a post that's already better than 90% of what's on LinkedIn. The move isn't to teach them anything. The move is to read their draft, say "this is ready," and make them post it before they leave the Zoom. If they argue, the real block isn't the draft. It's identity. They don't believe they're the person who's allowed to say this publicly yet. Cited from six months of 1-on-1 patterns across [[1_1_Sofia_2024_10_03]], [[Ross_1_1_2025_02_18]], and [[Anastasia_1_1_2024_09_20]].

**The Lost Beginner.** This person is early. Maybe pre-revenue. Maybe just post-launch. They signed up because the workshop sounded relevant and they don't want to miss out. But they're not READY for what you're teaching. You're talking about scaling to $500K and they haven't made their first <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-START::-->0K. You're talking about hiring a team and they're still doing all five jobs themselves. If you ignore them, they feel ripped off. If you slow the session down for them, the other four archetypes revolt. The solution isn't to exclude them. The solution is to SEGMENT the deliverable. Give them one thing to ship this week that moves them forward without requiring the infrastructure the others already have.

In Priyanka C's 6-week cadence workshop, this came up explicitly. She had three people at <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-END::-->00K+ ARR who needed advanced LinkedIn strategy and two people at $0 ARR who needed to figure out what they even sold. I told her to design two parallel tracks: the operators get the full 6-week content calendar, the beginners get a 1-week "prove the offer" sprint. Same Zoom, different homework. Cited from [[Priyanka_-6_week_cadenceworkshop_2026_04_09]].

The mistake most founders make is designing the ENTIRE workshop for archetype 3 or 4 because those are the ones who talk the most in the chat. Archetypes 1 and 2 sit quietly, get nothing useful, and don't come back. Archetype 5 gets overwhelmed and refunds.

## Specific examples from the vault

### Example 1: Angelin Shalini in Founder OS AI Masterclass

Angelin showed up to session 4 with a full team, a working content operation, and zero interest in "what AI is." She wanted one thing: compress her 12-hour weekly content workflow into 2 hours using Claude. By end of session she had a working Voice DNA document, a Content Strategist skill, and a Writer skill. She tested it live. It worked. She shipped a post the next day. That's the Ready-to-Ship Operator. No theory. No "let me think about it." Just: show me the system, I'll implement it, measure the result. Cited from [[Founder_OS_AI_Masterclass_4th_Session_2025_12_06]].

### Example 2: Bryant's Cashflow Inner Circle — the Stuck Strategist loop

Bryant runs a weekly group call for founders working on cash and ops. Same pattern every week: someone asks "should I raise my prices or add more volume?" I watch them spiral. They want the FORMULA. "If my margin is X and my CAC is Y, what's the right move?" The formula doesn't exist. The decision is always context-dependent. The breakthrough happens when I stop answering the question and force them to PICK ONE and test it for two weeks. No more analysis. No more "it depends." Pick one. Ship it. Measure. Adjust. The ones who pick one and ship make $50K more in 90 days. The ones who keep asking "but what if" are still asking the same question six months later. Cited from [[Cashflow_Inner_Circle_Session_2026_02_06]] and [[Cashflow_Inner_Circle_Session_2026_02_13]].

### Example 3: Franziska Niehus in Weekly Success Circle — Visibility Chaser pattern

Franziska could coach. She had clients. She had testimonials. Her LinkedIn profile was fine. But she posted once a month and nobody booked calls from it. The session that unlocked her wasn't about hooks or frameworks. It was about forcing her to write ONE post about a real client session she'd just had, name the client's actual problem in their words, and ship it that day. She did. Three people DM'd. One booked. That's the Visibility Chaser unlock: they don't need more training. They need to ship ONE thing publicly that's specific enough that their ICP recognises themselves in it. Cited from [[Weekly_Success_Circle_🌟_2023_09_25]] and [[Weekly_Success_Circle_🌟_2023_10_02]].

### Example 4: Sofia's confidence block — the Executor who won't ship

Sofia had written a LinkedIn post about her framework. It was GOOD. Clear hook. Real POV. Specific outcome. She sent it to me asking "is this okay?" I read it. It was better than 80% of what I see on LinkedIn. I told her "this is ready, post it now." She said "I want to think about it one more day." That's the tell. The draft isn't the problem. The identity is. She doesn't believe she's allowed to have a public POV yet. The move isn't to workshop the post. The move is to say "you're posting this today or I'm posting it for you." She posted it. It got 40 likes and 2 DMs. The next post took her 10 minutes instead of 3 days. Cited from [[1_1_Sofia_2024_10_03]].

### Example 5: Priyanka's 6-week cadence workshop — segmenting for the Lost Beginner

Priyanka was running a 6-week content calendar workshop. Five attendees. Three were at <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-END::-->00K+ ARR and needed advanced LinkedIn positioning. Two were pre-revenue and still figuring out what they sold. The pre-revenue two kept asking beginner questions ("how do I find my niche?" "should I use hashtags?") and the advanced three were visibly frustrated. I told Priyanka: segment the deliverable. Give the beginners a 1-week "validate your offer" sprint. One post. One DM to 10 people. One booked call. That's their homework. The advanced group gets the full 6-week calendar. Same Zoom. Different tracks. Cited from [[Priyanka_-6_week_cadenceworkshop_2026_04_09]].

### Example 6: NAS.IO AI Business School — the operations team archetype mix

When I worked with the NAS.IO team (Alex, Nuseir, Alexa, Matt) on session structure for their AI Business School curriculum, the attendee mix was WIDE. Some attendees were running <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-START::-->M+ creator businesses. Some were just starting. The mistake would've been designing one linear curriculum. The solution was modular: core sessions everyone attends, plus breakout tracks by revenue stage. Operators get the advanced automation stack. Beginners get the "first <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-START::-->0K" workflow. Everyone gets value. Nobody feels lost or bored. Cited from [[Nas.io document redesign with branding - 6b8ef772]].

## Scripts and exact phrases

**For the Ready-to-Ship Operator:**
"By end of this session you'll have a working system. If it's not working, we stay until it is."

**For the Stuck Strategist:**
"You don't need more information. You need to pick one path and test it for two weeks. What's your decision?"

**For the Visibility Chaser:**
"You don't have a skills problem. You have a shipping problem. Write one post about your last client session and publish it before you leave this call."

**For the Confidence-Blocked Executor:**
"This draft is ready. If you're waiting for it to feel perfect, it never will. Post it now or I'm posting it for you."

**For the Lost Beginner:**
"You're not ready for the advanced track yet, and that's fine. Here's your one-week sprint: prove someone will pay for this. One post, ten DMs, one booked call. That's the only deliverable."

**Universal script for segmenting deliverables:**
"Everyone gets a different homework assignment this week based on where you are. Operators: build the system. Beginners: validate the offer. Stuck strategists: make one decision by Friday."

**For setting expectations in the first 5 minutes:**
"There are five types of people in this room. Some of you are here to scale what's working. Some of you are here to fix what's stuck. Some of you are here to ship your first thing. I'm designing this session so all five of you get what you need. Your homework will be different."

## Common mistakes

**Mistake 1: Designing for the person who talks the most.**
The Visibility Chaser and the Confidence-Blocked Executor dominate the chat. They ask the most questions. They send the most follow-ups. If you design the entire workshop around them, the Ready-to-Ship Operator walks out after 20 minutes because nothing is actionable enough. The Stuck Strategist spirals deeper into analysis paralysis because you gave them MORE options instead of forcing a decision.

**Mistake 2: Giving everyone the same homework.**
If you tell the Lost Beginner to "build a 6-week content calendar," they'll stare at a blank Notion page for three days and give up. If you tell the Ready-to-Ship Operator to "validate your offer," they'll be insulted because they already have 20 paying clients. Segment the deliverable by archetype or half the room gets nothing useful.

**Mistake 3: Slowing the session down for the beginner.**
The beginner asks "what's an ICP?" in minute 15. If you stop and explain it, the other four archetypes check out. The solution isn't to ignore the beginner. The solution is to say "I'll drop a 2-minute Loom in the chat after this session. For now, assume ICP means the one person who would pay you <!--::DANNY-DISTILL-START::-->0K tomorrow if you solved their exact problem." Keep moving.

**Mistake 4: Letting the Stuck Strategist spiral.**
They ask "should I do X or Y?" You answer. They ask "but what if Z?" You answer. They ask "okay but how do I know?" This loop will eat 40 minutes if you let it. The breakthrough is to cut it off: "You're going to pick one and test it for two weeks. Which one?" If they say "I don't know," you pick for them. "Do X. Test it. If it fails, we'll adjust on the next call."

**Mistake 5: Praising the Confidence-Blocked Executor without forcing the ship.**
They show you a draft. It's good. You say "this is great!" They smile and… do nothing. Praise doesn't unlock them. Forcing the action does. The script is: "This is ready. Post it before you leave this Zoom or it won't happen."

**Mistake 6: Assuming the Ready-to-Ship Operator will "figure it out themselves."**
They will. But if they're PAYING you for a workshop, they expect you to compress three weeks of trial-and-error into 90 minutes. If your session is all theory and no system, they'll feel ripped off. The deliverable for this archetype must be a working tool, script, or workflow they can use Monday morning.

## The contrarian read

Most workshop advice says "know your audience" and "design for one specific ICP."

I say the opposite. Design for ALL five archetypes and segment the deliverable.

The conventional wisdom assumes that if you try to serve everyone, you serve no one. That's true for MARKETING. It's false for DELIVERY. Once someone has paid and shown up, they're not all the same person. They're five different people with five different readiness levels. If you deliver the same thing to all five, three walk away disappointed.

The other contrarian take: the best workshops don't teach. They TRIAGE.

Most founders design workshops like a masterclass: here's the framework, here's the steps, here's the examples, now go apply it. That works for exactly one archetype (the Visibility Chaser who needs a framework). It fails for the other four. The Ready-to-Ship Operator doesn't need steps. They need a working system. The Stuck Strategist doesn't need more options. They need ONE decision. The Confidence-Blocked Executor doesn't need examples. They need permission. The Lost Beginner doesn't need the full framework. They need the first 10% that proves the concept.

A well-designed workshop is a triage session. You diagnose which of the five archetypes each person is in the first 10 minutes, then you give them the ONE intervention that moves them forward. Everyone gets a different prescription. Same room, different medicine.

## What it looks like when it's working

Post-session Slack stays active for weeks, not days. Each archetype is asking DIFFERENT questions because they're working on different deliverables.

Testimonials stop being generic ("great session!") and start being specific by outcome. The operator says "I compressed 12 hours to 2." The visibility chaser says "I posted consistently for the first time in six months." The confidence blocker says "I shipped the offer I'd been sitting on for a year."

Alumni retention hits 70%+ for the next cohort. When someone gets the exact intervention they needed, they come back. When they feel invisible, they don't.

Refund requests drop to near zero. Refunds happen when expectations don't match delivery. If you SET the expectation in minute 1 that different people will get different homework, nobody is surprised when their deliverable looks different from the person next to them.

Breakout rooms become productive instead of awkward. When you pair a Ready-to-Ship Operator with a Confidence-Blocked Executor, the operator just TELLS them to ship it and the blocker does. When you pair two Stuck Strategists, they spiral together and nothing moves. Architecting the breakout rooms by archetype changes the energy in the room.

The number that matters most: how many people shipped something DURING the session or within 24 hours. If that number is below 50%, your workshop was a lecture. If it's above 80%, you triaged correctly.
